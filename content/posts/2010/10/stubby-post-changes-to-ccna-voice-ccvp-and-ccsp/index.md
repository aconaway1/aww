I don't usually cover news from Cisco, but they've changed some [certification](http://www.cisco.com/web/learning/le3/learning_career_certifications_and_learning_paths_home.html) stuff around again, and I thought I would bring it up.  This time they've changed the CCNA Voice, CCVP, and CCSP, so, if you've on those tracks, be careful what you're studying!

### **CCNA Voice**

Circle 28 February 2011 on your calendars.  That's when the [CCNA Voice](http://www.cisco.com/web/learning/le3/le2/le0/le3/learning_certification_type_home.html) track gets a shakeup.  The IIUC (640-460) exam will be no more, and passing CVOICE (642-436) will no longer be a valid way to get the cert.  After the big day, you'll have to take [ICOMM](https://learningnetwork.cisco.com/community/certifications/voice_ccna/icomm) [(640-461)](https://learningnetwork.cisco.com/community/certifications/voice_ccna/icomm).  This seems to be a much broader exam instead of having the enterprise and commercial focuses in CVOICE and IIUC, respectively.  Look out for both CME- and CUCM-based topics including a troubleshooting section.

### **CCVP**

The CCVP is now known as the methods.  The TUC exam is replaced by [TVOICE (642-427)](https://learningnetwork.cisco.com/community/certifications/ccvp/tvoice?tab=overview), which, on the surface seems to be just an update.  The [CIPT1 (642-447)](https://learningnetwork.cisco.com/community/certifications/ccvp/cipt1v8?tab=overview), and [CIPT2 (642-457)](https://learningnetwork.cisco.com/community/certifications/ccvp/cipt2v8?tab=overview) exams also look like they're simply updated, but you'll have to ask a Voice guy since I don't really know the differences here..  The last exam is [CAPPS (642-467)](https://learningnetwork.cisco.com/community/certifications/ccvp/capps?tab=overview), and covers Unity, VPIM, and Presence.  Fun stuff.

See also:

**CCSP**

Like the Voice track, the CCSP gets a name change and is now known as the [CCNP Security](http://www.cisco.com/web/learning/le3/le2/le37/le9/learning_certification_type_home.html).  There are still four tests like the old track, but the content is updated.  You have to take the [SECURE (642-637)](https://learningnetwork.cisco.com/community/certifications/ccnpsecurity/secure?tab=overview), [FIREWALL (642-617)](https://learningnetwork.cisco.com/community/certifications/ccnpsecurity/firewall?tab=overview), [VPN (642-647)](https://learningnetwork.cisco.com/community/certifications/ccnpsecurity/vpn?tab=overview), and  [IPS (642-627)](https://learningnetwork.cisco.com/community/certifications/ccnpsecurity/ips?tab=overview).  Word on the street is that the new VPN exam eliminates the inconsistencies with VPN deployment methods taught in SNAF and SNAA.
